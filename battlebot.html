Overview

Fred is a compact competition robot focused on delivering high kinetic energy impacts with repeatable reliability. The design emphasizes energy transfer efficiency, quick serviceability, and survivability in bracket-style matches.

Performance Highlights

  • Peak spin speed: ~8,000 RPM (measured)
  • Approx. 3 lb (weight-legal with 0.8 oz margin)
  • Modular weapon assembly for rapid swapouts

Build Details

Mechanical Design

Chassis & Structure

  • Low-profile 3D-printed chassis with integrated ribbing for stiffness
  • Aluminum and UHMW armor layout to deflect hits and protect internals
  • Optimized mass distribution to lower moment of inertia around drive axis

Weapon & Drive

  • Brushless motor driving a tapered titanium spinner
  • Custom motor mount with shear-pin safety and easy-access bearings
  • Independent two-wheel drive using high-torque DC gearmotors

Electronics & Controls

  • ESC + receiver for weapon control; robust power distribution with polyfuse protection
  • Battery: 2S LiPo (2200 mAh) optimized for burst power
  • Wiring harness designed for quick field repairs

Testing & Iteration

Testing focused on impact survivability and spin-up time. Iterations included reinforcement of the hub, new balancing procedures for the spinner, and improved bite geometry on the weapon edge.

Lessons Learned

  • Balancing at sub-milligram precision drastically reduced vibration-related failures
  • Modular armor allows rapid on-site repair between matches
  • Torque calculations and energy models helped predict damage thresholds
